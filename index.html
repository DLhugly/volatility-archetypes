Why This Is Different

What existing models can't do

Every vol desk runs GARCH, EWMA, and implied vol surfaces. Those answer one question: how much volatility? This system answers a different question: what kind — and is it about to change?

The 13 features are derived from cross-disciplinary physical science. None appear in any existing volatility model or published finance paper. The six regime types were discovered empirically — not predefined — and held stable across 69 assets, 9 asset classes, and 36 years of history.

Regime A
Pressure-Building
Volatility driven by deviation from equilibrium. Quiet on the surface, building toward a threshold break.
Regime B
Chain-Reaction
Volatility driven by persistence of extreme moves. One large move breeds more. Momentum in volatility.
Regime C
Scale-Divergence
Volatility driven by disagreement between fast and slow time scales. They always converge — the question is when.
Regime D
Boundary
Volatility near a regime transition. High probability of switching states. A large move is likely — direction unknown.
Regime E
Event-Sensitive
Volatility driven by directional asymmetry and external catalysts. Active around events, quiet between.
Regime F
Unstructured
No dominant mechanism detected. Driven by narrative and sentiment. Not exploitable from price alone.

Known limitations — full transparency

The system performs best during sharp, multi-day regime transitions (SVB +19pp, 2011 Euro +16pp, COVID +10pp). It performs at or near random during slow grinds (2022 rate hike +0pp, 2025 tariffs -10pp) and single-day flash events (2024 yen carry -8pp, 2018 Volmageddon +0pp). It is a seismograph for regime breaks, not a thermometer for gradual drift.
