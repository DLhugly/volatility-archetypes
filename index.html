Why This Is Different

What existing models can't do

How It Works

A weather forecast for financial volatility

Standard models measure what volatility was. This system measures what state the market is in — and whether that state is about to change. It classifies any asset, at any frequency, into one of six regime types using 13 proprietary features that no existing model incorporates.

Regime A
Pressure-Building
Volatility driven by deviation from equilibrium. Quiet on the surface, building invisible tension toward a threshold break.
Regime B
Chain-Reaction
Volatility driven by persistence of extreme moves. One large move breeds more. Momentum in volatility itself.
Regime C
Scale-Divergence
Volatility driven by disagreement between fast and slow time scales. They always converge — the question is when and which direction.
Regime D
Boundary
Volatility near a regime transition point. High probability of switching states. A significant move is likely — direction unknown.
Regime E
Event-Sensitive
Volatility driven by directional asymmetry and external catalysts. Active around events, quiet between them.
Regime F
Unstructured
No dominant volatility mechanism detected. Driven by narrative, sentiment, and idiosyncratic factors outside the price data.
